Intersectionality

Intersectionality is the combination of various traits such as race, age, class, and gender that make up an individual and the bias a person receives based on these traits. The heart of intersectionality is that each individual has a unique experience because everyone has a unique set of traits and circumstances that cumulatively define them. This term was popularized by Kimberlé Crenshaw, a critical race theory scholar and activist. The following video is a lecture explaining intersectionality.

Implicit Bias

Implicit bias is unconscious prejudice held by an individual in regard to a specific group, which may influence attitudes and actions toward that group. These biases are often shaped by learned associations and social norms. Everyone holds implicit biases related to gender, race, age, and other traits. In class, we took implicit bias tests to learn more about our own assumptions. You can take a similar test by clicking the link below. As Hockett explains, “If we want to move to a radically different place, a radically better place, on issues of race and difference in this country, we have to pay attention to something called implicit bias.”
